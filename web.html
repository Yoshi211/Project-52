Camels

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat

Pigeons

  1. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds
  2. Pigeons can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to
  3. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet and at an average speed of 77.6 mph

Cicadas

  1. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans
  2. More than 3,000 species exist
  3. Their wings repel water and bacteria

Pandas

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  2. An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day
  3. Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago

Bears

  1. Bears can cover short distances at speeds of up to 50 km/h
  2. Their sense of smell enables them to sniff food at a distance of several kilometres
  3. Bears in the wild are mainly active at dusk and at night.

Chameleons

  1. They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature
  2. Skin crystals enable them to change color at will
  3. Chameleons can't regrow their tail
